Alkenes and Alkynes

In this lesson, we will learn about two important classes of hydrocarbons: alkenes and alkynes. Alkenes are hydrocarbons that contain at least one double bond, while alkynes contain at least one triple bond. Both are unsaturated hydrocarbons, which means they have fewer hydrogen atoms than alkanes.

Characteristics of Alkenes

Alkenes are characterized by the presence of at least one double bond between carbon atoms. This double bond gives alkenes different chemical properties compared to alkanes, such as being more reactive. The general formula for alkenes is CnH2n.

Characteristics of Alkynes

Alkynes, on the other hand, contain at least one triple bond between carbon atoms. This triple bond makes alkynes even more reactive than alkenes. The general formula for alkynes is CnH2n-2.
